Not a local student?

RMIT considers you a local student if you are:

  • a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • a New Zealand citizen, or
  • a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

You’re viewing program information for international students.

Not an international student?

RMIT considers you an international student if you are:

  • intending to study on a student visa, or
  • not a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • not a New Zealand citizen, or
  • not a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

How to apply

This information will assist you to review your eligibility and to prepare your application for Masters by Research or PhD at RMIT University.

This information will assist you to review your eligibility and to prepare your application for Masters by Research or PhD at RMIT University.

Research programs are offered at two levels: masters and doctorate. The duration to complete a doctorate is 4 years full time and masters by research is 2 years full time on average (or the equivalent part-time).

We also offer a range of tuition fee and stipend scholarships to support you through to the completion of your Graduate Research Degree.

  • Step 1. Important dates and information

    Your application will go through the admission to candidature and/or scholarship selection process once submitted.

    Step 1. Important dates and information

  • Step 2. Entry requirements

    RMIT admits applicants on the basis of their demonstrated capacity to conduct independent research.

    Step 2. Entry requirements

  • Step 3. Identify your research topic

    All applicants should have a proposed research topic that is aligned with at least one of RMIT's identified research strengths.

    Step 3. Identify your research topic

  • Step 4. Find a supervisory team

    You must then find a supervisory team to support your application

    Step 4. Find a supervisory team

  • Step 5. Prepare your proposal

    After finding a potential supervisory team, you must develop a research proposal.

    Step 5. Prepare your proposal

  • Step 6. Apply for admission and scholarship

    Once you have secured a supervisory team to support your proposal you can apply.

    Step 6. Apply for admission and scholarship
