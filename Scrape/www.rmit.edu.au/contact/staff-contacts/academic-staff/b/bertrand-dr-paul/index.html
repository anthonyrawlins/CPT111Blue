Not a local student?

RMIT considers you a local student if you are:

  • a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • a New Zealand citizen, or
  • a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

Not an international student?

RMIT considers you an international student if you are:

  • intending to study on a student visa, or
  • not a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • not a New Zealand citizen, or
  • not a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

Overview

Reasearch overview

Dr Paul Bertrand's research focuses on the neuronal basis of functional bowel diseases and the neurophysiology of the peripheral nervous system in conditions such as obesity, inflammation and aging.  We focus on the roles of ATP and serotonin (5-HT) in neurotransmission and sensory transduction using electrophysiological and electrochemical techniques.

Specifically, Dr Bertrand is looking at:

  1. The function and regulation of the serotonin transporter SERT during obesity or inflammation
  2. Regulation of serotonin from EC cells by TRP and pannexin channels in health and during disease
  3. Neuroplasticity of enteric neurons and neurotransmission by P2X and 5-HT3 channel/receptor complexes

Dr Bertrand ’s laboratory is based in the School Medical Sciences and is affiliated with the Health Innovations Research Institute.

Please see Google scholar for a complete list of publications by Dr Paul Bertrand.

Key activities

Dr Bertrand is a senior lecturer at RMIT University in the School of Medical Sciences. He coordinates a number of courses and in 2014 took over as the HDR coordinator for Medical Sciences. He has a productive research laboratory which focuses on the neural control of the digestive system and supervises Honours and PhD students.
