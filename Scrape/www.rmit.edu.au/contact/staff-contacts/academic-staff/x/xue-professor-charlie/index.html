Not a local student?

RMIT considers you a local student if you are:

  • a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • a New Zealand citizen, or
  • a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

Professor Charlie C. Xue is Head of School, Health and Biomedical Sciences at RMIT University. He provides academic and research leadership in the School.

Expand all sections

Overview

Since 2011, Professor Xue has led the review of the School’s academic programs, research priorities, and governance structure. The School was reviewed by an external expert Panel in 2012 which resulted in the development of the School’s Vision and Mission:

Vision: To be a leader in education and research in evidence-based healthcare (EBH) with a focus on the ageing population and chronic diseases.

Mission: To provide high quality health sciences education and high impact research that contribute to the provision of EBH to the community.

Under the leadership of Professor Xue, significant effort has been made to improve teaching quality and efficiency, research capacity and output, as well as School governance guided by the “One School Approach“.

Outside the School, Professor Xue contributes to the strategic direction setting of the College of Science Engineering and Health as a member of the College Executive. Professor Xue works collaboratively with other members of the College Executive for the planning and development of academic programs, cross-disciplinary research and commercial activities. He also contributes to the RMIT University’s Academic Board, Research Committee, College Board and Academic Promotion Review Panels.

Professor Xue also holds the following appointments:

  • Director, Traditional & Complementary Medicine [TCM] Research Program, RMIT Health Innovations Research Institute
  • Grant Review Panel, the National Health and Medical Research Council [NHMRC] (2011-2013)
  • Senior Principal Research Fellow, Guangdong Provincial Academy of Chinese Medical Sciences, China
  • National Chair, Chinese Medicine Board of Australia, an appointment by the Australian Health Workforce Ministerial Council
  • Member, World Health Organization (WHO) Expert Advisory Panel for Traditional Medicine (appointed by Director-General of the WHO, Geneva
  • Chair, Terminology Committee, International Classification of Traditional Medicine; and Member, Project Advisory Group, WHO, Geneva
  • Director, WHO Collaborating Centre for Traditional Medicine, RMIT University
  • Director, Chinese Medicine Confucius Institute, RMIT University

From 1995 to 2010, Professor Charlie Xue was the Foundation Discipline Head (Chinese Medicine) at RMIT. Professor Xue has organized numerous major academic conferences/ Symposia, visits by senior government officials from several countries as well as international organizations such as the World Health Organisation (WHO).

Research interests

Professor Xue's main research interests are evidence-based medicine and translational research with a focus on Complementary and Alternative Medicine (CAM, acupuncture and herbal medicine) for chronic respiratory diseases and pain management. His team consists of six postdoctoral fellows and fifteen PhD candidates. He has established extensive links nationally and internationally in traditional medicine research. Professor Xue’s research has been supported by over AU$15 million of research grants including six Australian National Health and Medical Research Council (NHMRC) Project Grants, two Australian Research Council (ARC) Linkage Grants, and more than 30 Federal and State Government Grants, and international research grants.

Media contributions

Since 1995, Professor Xue has contributed to over 250 media interviews to radio, TV, newspapers, reports and professional journals nationally and internationally. He has provided expert comments in education, clinical research and regulation concerning acupuncture and herbal medicine. The majority of his scientific media contribution has been on chronic respiratory diseases and pain management. In 2009, Professor Xue was a finalist of the RMIT Research Media Star.
