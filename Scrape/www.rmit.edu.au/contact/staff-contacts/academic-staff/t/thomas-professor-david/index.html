Not a local student?

RMIT considers you a local student if you are:

  • a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • a New Zealand citizen, or
  • a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

You’re viewing program information for international students.

Not an international student?

RMIT considers you an international student if you are:

  • intending to study on a student visa, or
  • not a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • not a New Zealand citizen, or
  • not a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

Overview

  • Professor of Fine Art (Painting/ Installation)
  • Research
  • Postgraduate supervision
  • International and research committee work

David was born in Belfast Northern Ireland in 1951 and arrived in Australia in 1958.

His work is represented in private and public collections in Australia, USA, France, Germany, NZ, and the UK.

David has received numerous grants from Arts Victoria, Australia Council. Numerous residencies including AGNSW studio Cite International des Arts Paris France 2000.

Awards

In 2001 David received the Certificate of Achievement in Research Supervision in the RMIT University Award for Excellence in Teaching. Since 1992, he has successfully supervised 43 postgraduate research candidates to timely completion.

Research

Leader of Art Time and Space Research cluster SOA 2004-11

David's work is informed by both Eastern and Western monochrome painting.

In combining and juxtaposing monochrome painting with photography, his work addresses ideas of the real and the transitory, the spatial and the temporal. He creates contemplative works that celebrate colour and feeling, challenging the perception of both photography and painting, asking the viewer to consider their conceptual ideas and experiential responses.
