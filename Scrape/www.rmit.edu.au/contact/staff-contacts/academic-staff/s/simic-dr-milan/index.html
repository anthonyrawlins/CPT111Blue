Not a local student?

RMIT considers you a local student if you are:

  • a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • a New Zealand citizen, or
  • a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

You’re viewing program information for international students.

Not an international student?

RMIT considers you an international student if you are:

  • intending to study on a student visa, or
  • not a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • not a New Zealand citizen, or
  • not a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

Dr Milan Simic is the Program Manager, Masters of Engineering Management and associate Editor, International Journal of Knowledge Engineering and Soft Data Paradigms. He is a Silver Member of KES International and a member of the Australia-India Research Centre for Automation Software Engineering, RMIT UAS Research team, and RMIT Design Institute.

Overview

Dr Milan Simic has comprehensive experience from industry, R&D institute and academia, from overseas and Australia. He is currently the Program Manager for the Masters of Engineering (Management). Prior to that he was the Program Director for the Advanced Manufacturing and Mechatronics program. His contribution has been the introduction, accreditation, management and the delivery of the Mechatronics program at RMIT University.

Dr Simic is an Associate Director for the Australia-India Research Centre for Automation Software Engineering (AIRCAUSE). AICAUSE is a joint initiative of RMIT University, the State Government of Victoria and the ABB Group (India and Australia).

In addition to management, Dr Simic is conducting teaching and research, currently lecturing mechatronics, electronics and management subjects in the School of Aerospace, Mechanical & Manufacturing Engineering at RMIT University. He has more than eighty (80+) research publications including 5 books. His publications cover international journals and conference proceedings from the areas of Digital Communications, Electronics and Mechatronics Engineering, as well as Engineering Education.

In June 2015 Dr Simic’s research team, that has developed fast 3D metal printing technology, won an international award in Germany. The Bosch Venture Forum Award is presented to new initiatives and start-up businesses across the world each year by Robert Bosch Venture Capital (RBVC).

The article “In the Passenger Seat: Investigating Ride Comfort Measures in Autonomous Cars”, written by Elbanhawi, M.; Simic, M.; Jazar, R. is the most popular article in Intelligent Transportation Systems Magazine, IEEE, for 2015.

For his contributions, Dr Simic has also received other prestigious awards and recognitions: Two for the research and development from Honeywell and two RMIT University awards for the excellence in teaching and the provision of education to the community. He has also received a number of innovation projects’ grants and various research grants.

Dr Simic’s research interests and activities are multidisciplinary and include following scientific areas:

  • Robotics, Autonomous Systems, Intelligent Transport Systems, Path Planning
  • Information Theory: Coding and Modulation, Line Coding, Run Length Limited (RLL) Coding
  • Wireless Communications, Long Term Evolution (LTE) Air Interfaces
  • Active Safety Systems in Automotive Engineering based on vehicle to vehicle communications and sensor networks, VANET (Vehicle Ad-Hoc Networking)
  • Physical systems modelling, Green energy production
  • Biomedical Engineering, Biotechnologies
  • RAID (Redundant Array of Independent Disks) R&D conducted in Bull HN (Bull, Honeywell, NEC)
