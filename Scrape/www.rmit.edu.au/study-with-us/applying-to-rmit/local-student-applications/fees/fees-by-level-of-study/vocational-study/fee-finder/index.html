Not a local student?

RMIT considers you a local student if you are:

  • a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • a New Zealand citizen, or
  • a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

You’re viewing program information for international students.

Not an international student?

RMIT considers you an international student if you are:

  • intending to study on a student visa, or
  • not a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • not a New Zealand citizen, or
  • not a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

Vocational education fee finder

Use the fee finder to get an estimate of fees for your vocational education program.

You can find your program code in your RMIT offer. Alternatively, program codes are displayed at the top of each program summary, which you can find in Study with us.

Important: These fees are estimates only, apply only to local students, and may be subject to change. 

Find fees for your vocational education program

How much you will pay depends on what type of fee paying place you are offered.

Victorian Government subsidised students: Eligibility is based on previous study and is also subject to providing acceptable evidence of your citizenship and age, as detailed in the Evidence of student eligibility student declaration form.

Concession students: You may be entitled to a concession on your tuition and student services and amenities fees if you are in a Victorian Government subsidised place and you meet the eligibility criteria to apply for a concession.

Full-fee students: If you are offered a full-fee place you will pay the full tuition costs of your program.

Further information

  • These fees are estimates only. The student tuition fees as published are subject to change given individual circumstances at enrolment. 
  • Fees are adjusted on an annual basis and the fees in your quote apply only to the year selected.
  • Students are invoiced each semester based on their enrolled courses for that semester. Fees per semester can be calculated by multiplying the nominal hours of your intended enrolment with the hourly rate for your fee place as listed above.
  • Tuition fees do not include include the student services and amenities fee (SSAF) and materials fees that may apply.
  • Materials fees include fees and charges associated with field trips or goods that are used to produce items that become your physical property. These fees are not compulsory and you may choose to purchase these items independently.
  • Nominal hours represent a guide to the relative teaching time and student effort required to successfully achieve a particular competency/module. This may include not only scheduled classes or workplace visits but also the amount of effort required to undertake, evaluate and complete all assessment requirements, including any non-classroom activities. 
  • The duration shown is the standard duration for the program. However, RMIT may choose to deliver the program in a shorter length of time and if this occurs the total tuition fee for the program will remain the same. 

The Victorian Government contribution to your tuition fees is paid directly to RMIT. Students are invoiced exclusive of the contribution.
