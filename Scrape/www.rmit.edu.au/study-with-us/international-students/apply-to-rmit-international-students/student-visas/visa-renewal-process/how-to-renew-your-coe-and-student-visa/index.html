Not a local student?

RMIT considers you a local student if you are:

  • a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • a New Zealand citizen, or
  • a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

Not an international student?

RMIT considers you an international student if you are:

  • intending to study on a student visa, or
  • not a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • not a New Zealand citizen, or
  • not a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

How to renew your CoE and student visa

Process for renewing your CoE and student visa

Step 1

Read DIBP’s information about renewing your student visa.

Information about applying for a new student visa can be found on the Department of Immigration and Border Protection's website. This page gives you some very useful information about your visa and its expiry date. It is very important that you are aware of your visa expiry date and that you apply for a new visa before your current visa expires. For further information, please go to the Department's Extending Your Stay page.

Step 2

Download a Visa Application Document Checklist.

This checklist gives you information about the supporting documentation you are required to lodge with your student visa application. It also lists additional documents that you may need to provide depending on your individual circumstances.

Step 3

Apply for a new  Confirmation of enrolment (CoE).

You need to indicate why you are applying for an extension and you must attach supporting documentation.

It is important that you complete the Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) Request Form and lodge this form at least 10 working days before you need to use your new CoE.

You must ensure that section B on this form is completed by your School before lodging it with the International Office. Your application cannot be processed until the School has completed section B.

Your application for a new CoE will take approximately 10 working days to process.

Your new CoE will be emailed to you.

Step 4

Renew your Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) if necessary. 

Your OSHC needs to be valid until the end of your course. This means that you must have visa-length OSHC at all times. 

Please read the Health insurance for students page on the DIBP website.

Please contact your OSHC provider for assistance with renewing your cover.

Medibank
Phone:  132 331 or 0061 3 8622 5780 (if you are outside Australia)
Email:  ask_us@medibank.com.au

BUPA
Phone: 1800 888 942 or 0061 2 9461 3850 (if you are outside Australia)

Worldcare
Phone: 136 742
Email: oshc@worldcare.com.au

NOTE:

You should lodge your visa application within 45 days of the date your CoE is issued, otherwise it will expire and you will not be able to use it. Also make sure that you lodge your visa application BEFORE the course start date listed on your CoE. RMIT will not issue a new CoE if you fail to lodge your application in time.

Step 5

Lodge your visa application online or by post.

Applying online

Students who are applying to renew their visa in Australia are strongly encouraged to lodge their visa application online. If you are applying online, you will need a credit card to pay for the application fee.  When have completed your application, you will be issued with a Transaction Reference Number (TRN) to confirm that your application has been received by DIBP.

The Immigration website provides information about renewing your visa online. Please read this information carefully and contact DIBP directly if you have any questions. 

If you want to apply online, please refer to the link to your relevant student visa subclass for information about the application process and access to online visa lodgement.

Subclass 570 visa application

Subclass 572 visa application

Subclass 573 visa application

Subclass 574 visa application

Subclass 575 visa application

We strongly recommend that you lodge your visa application onshore in Australia. Lodging an application overseas may result in delays that prevent you returning on time.

Important things to note:

  1. Complete applications: ensure that your application is complete. If you lodge an incomplete application, your visa may be refused.
  2. Passport details: Your visa will be linked to the passport number you use in your application. You must use the same passport to travel to and from Australia. Your passport should be valid for at least 6 months at the time you lodge your visa application.
  3. Application charge: You will be required to pay a non-refundable application charge. Use the visa pricing calculator to estimate the amount your visa will cost you.
  4. Other costs. There may be other costs associated with applying for a new visa. These may include translating documents in to English (if required) and any medical examinations you need. Your visa medicals are not covered under your OSHC. If requested, you may have to provide a Police check. The cost of the Police check will be your responsibility.

Step 6

Medical Check

Read the information about the health process for visa applicants before you lodge your visa application. This will advise you about the process of obtaining a medical for visa purposes. 

If a health examination is required, book an appointment for a medical examination and X-Ray with Medibank Health Solutions.

If you need to make an appointment with Medibank Health Solutions, follow these steps:

  1. Download and complete the "My Health Declaration" DIBP form required for a medical examination. Print out the completed for. This is your 'referral letter". You will need this letter, which includes a HAP ID number, to make an appointment if required.
  2. Make an appointment with Medibank Health Solutions. You can make an appointment online or by phone at 1300 361 046 or 9244 8381. You will need a credit card and a valid email address.

Appointments can only be made five days in advance.

NOTE

For more information about renewing your student visa visit the Extending your stay page on the DIPB website at http://www.border.gov.au/Trav/Stud/More/Extending-Your-Stay
