    This is an apprenticeship program. Through structured training, you will acquire the skills and knowledge to work in the dry cleaning industry.

    As a dry cleaner, you will classify articles to be dry cleaned, wet washed or spotted, looking for stains, damage, colour and types of material. You will use specialised techniques and equipment to clean clothes, fabrics and finish garments using various presses.

    You will gain hands-on skills in operating specialist machines, and gain an understanding of various cleaning applications, production techniques and customer service.

    This program will also develop your skills and knowledge in the operations of dry cleaning enterprises.

    Expand all sections

    Duration

    Certificate II: 1.5 years full-time or 3 years part-time.

    Certificate III: 3 years full-time or 6 years part-time.

    Training is conducted in the workplace. Students may enter at either Certificate II or Certificate III level.

    Duration

    Multiple campuses

    Learning and teaching

    RMIT offers a variety of learning and teaching approaches that give you direct insight and experience into the global fashion and textiles industries. Your practical experience and training is divided between your employer and RMIT.

    Training is tailored to the needs of the company, enterprise and the trainee. This provides a flexible and dynamic approach to your training needs and the needs of the company.

    You will be trained through your normal workplace practices by your employer. RMIT trainers will visit you regularly in the workplace to carry out and facilitate the award-based training. RMIT trainers have current industry experience and up-to-date knowledge of the market and the latest trends and innovations.

    You will predominantly study in the workplace and undertake occasional workshops at the well-equipped facilities at RMIT's Brunswick campus.

    When on campus you will work on industry standard equipment such as:

    • perchloroethylene and hydrocarbon dry cleaning machines
    • wet washing machines
    • spotting tables
    • various presses
    • point of sale customer service counter.

    Ongoing assessment throughout the semester includes practical demonstrations and observation and practical tasks.

    You can access online resources through the myRMIT student portal.

    All programs are delivered in English.

    Industry connections

    RMIT University is committed to providing you with an education that strongly links formal learning with professional or vocational practice.



    Career outlook

    As a dry cleaner, you will classify articles to be dry cleaned, wet washed or spotted, looking for stains, damage, colour and types of material. You will use specialised techniques and equipment to clean clothes, fabrics and finish garments using various presses.

    This program will prepare you for several roles within a dry cleaning plant, such as:

    • dry cleaner
    • presser
    • customer service attendant.

    Professional recognition

    These certificates are nationally accredited and are recognised by the The Drycleaning Institute of Australia Limited (DIA)

    Program structure

    Local students International students Change

    You will study either 2 years full-time or 4 years part-time.

    This program is delivered through:

    • on-the-job training in the workplace delivered by RMIT staff
    • manuals, learning materials and practical exercises
    • occasional on-campus RMIT workshops.
    • competency-based training and assessment.

    The full Certificate III—apprenticeship program incorporates the following courses:

    • OH&S policies and procedures
    • Apply quality standards
    • Work in the TCF industry
    • Identify fibres and fabrics
    • Receive and sort
    • Identify cleaning requirements
    • Operate dry cleaning machines
    • Operate finishing equipment
    • Identify pre and post spotting requirements
    • Conduct safe handling
    • Organise and plan own work
    • Control production
    • Provide customer service
    • Operate wet cleaning machines
    • Perform spotting functions
    • Dry clean special treatments
    • Carry out final inspection
    • Prepare articles for dispatch
    • Participate in sustainable work.

    If your application for a fee concession is approved, you will be charged 20% of the standard government subsidised tuition fee for your program. You will also be charged a lower rate on your student services and amenities fee (SSAF).

    Additional expenses

    In addition to tuition fees, you will be charged an annual student services and amenities fee (SSAF), which is used to maintain and enhance services and amenities that improve your experience as an RMIT student.

    The SSAF is calculated based on your enrolment load and the maximum fee for 2016 is $290.

    For more information about calculating your actual SSAF see Paying SSAF.

    You may also be required to purchase other items related to your program, including field trips, textbooks and equipment. These additional fees and expenses vary from program to program.
