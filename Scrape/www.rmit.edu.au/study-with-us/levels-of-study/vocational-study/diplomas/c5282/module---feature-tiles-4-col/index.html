Not a local student?

RMIT considers you a local student if you are:

  • a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • a New Zealand citizen, or
  • a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

You’re viewing program information for international students.

Not an international student?

RMIT considers you an international student if you are:

  • intending to study on a student visa, or
  • not a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • not a New Zealand citizen, or
  • not a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

    Jason Calvin has great respect for the medical industry and hopes his qualification in Laboratory Technology will lead him down the path of making worthy contributions to it.

    Job title and organisation:
    RMIT program:

    Diploma in Laboratory Technology (Biotechnology)
    Scholarship or opportunity:
    Research field:
    Country of origin:
    Destination:

    I’d encourage students considering this program to apply because the courses are well planned, the staff are helpful, and the practicals are fascinating to learn about and perform.

    I’ve always been interested in science, particularly biochemistry, and I want to work in an industry that contributes to people’s health and well-being.

    I chose RMIT because it’s a reputable university that sets high standards for its students. It also offers many activities for personal development, which makes us more employable.

    This program teaches you to follow detailed instructions, which is important because we deal with dangerous chemicals and chemical reactions. As quality control is a major component of our industry, you’re taught to take thorough notes, so that any errors can be traced back to the source.

    I’ve gained a whole suite of skills including analytical skills, abstract thinking, sturdy hand skills for pipetting and titration as well as how to select appropriate tests, to name a few.

    My favourite subject is Perform Microbiological Tests because it’s a fascinating field, and I get to perform tests that identify microorganisms.

    Eventually, I hope to complete a science degree, such as biomedical science. But for now, I want to work as a laboratory technician and progress to a senior position, ideally in forensics.

    I’d encourage students considering this program to apply because the courses are well planned, the staff are helpful, and the practicals are fascinating to learn about and perform.

    I love what I do because it enables me to contribute to the discovery of medical solutions for diseases and biological conditions.

    Stefania Corneschi enjoys delving into the inner-workings of biological materials, so the Diploma of Laboratory Technology was the perfect choice for her.

    Job title and organisation:
    RMIT program:

    Diploma of Laboratory Techonology (Biotechnology)
    Scholarship or opportunity:
    Research field:
    Country of origin:
    Destination:

    My favourite subject is biology - it’s fascinating to learn how every living thing is made up of these tiny machines that work together non-stop in perfect unison to make us, us.

    I started another science program at RMIT which wasn’t what I’d expected but it made me realise the area I’d like to pursue, so I enrolled in the Diploma of Laboratory Technology. The courses were more related to what I was interested in.

    I chose to study at RMIT because it offers the most diverse selection of science programs.

    This is a very hands-on program and everything we do is related back to how and why it’s relevant in the industry. It also sets you up for higher education if you want to further your studies. That being said, it’s not easy, but it is fun. I’ve gained skills in team work, time management and critical thinking to name just a few.

    My favourite subject is biology - it’s fascinating to learn how every living thing is made up of these tiny machines that work together non-stop in perfect unison to make us, us.

    The highlight of my studies has been the friends I’ve made. The small class sizes made it easier to get to know people.

    My plan is to finish my program and then find work in a research lab. I’d like to be part of a team that makes the next major biological or medical breakthrough that improves our understanding of disease.

    I love what I do because it really puts into perspective how important the world of biotechnology is to understanding life around us.
