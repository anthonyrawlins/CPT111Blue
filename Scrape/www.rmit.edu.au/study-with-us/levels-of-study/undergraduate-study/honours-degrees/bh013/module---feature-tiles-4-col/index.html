Not a local student?

RMIT considers you a local student if you are:

  • a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • a New Zealand citizen, or
  • a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

You’re viewing program information for international students.

Not an international student?

RMIT considers you an international student if you are:

  • intending to study on a student visa, or
  • not a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • not a New Zealand citizen, or
  • not a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

    A highlight of the Bachelor of Computer Science for Shae Whitehead, has been meeting a bunch of new people, and gaining a new perspective on what goes on in computer science.

    Job title and organisation:
    RMIT program:

    Bachelor of Computer Science
    Scholarship or opportunity:
    Research field:
    Country of origin:
    Destination: United Kingdom

    I was attracted to Computer Science as the intricacies and delicate nature of computers and their systems intrigue me. RMIT is great as I really enjoy the atmosphere and how social it is. There’s always something going on.

    I was attracted to Computer Science as the intricacies and delicate nature of computers and their systems intrigue me.

    RMIT is great as I really enjoy the atmosphere and how social it is. There’s always something going on.

    A highlight of my studies has been meeting a bunch of new people, and gaining a new perspective on what goes on in computer science.

    I’ve gained a lot of time management skills, as well as many social skills and a lot of patience.

    I have enjoyed studying the subject ‘Discrete structures in computing’ – although it’s a mathematics subject, the structure is set out well, and the lectures are always really interesting.

    I think that it is important for new students to keep an open mind as they enter this course. It’s a bunch of fun. It is also important to make the most of the extra assistance and clubs that RMIT can offer; they’ll come in handy if you ever get stuck with anything!

    My plan for the future is travel. Definitely travel, I want to see the world and gain a perspective of how my course relates to the rest of the world.

    25 Nov 2015

    Computer science researcher Dr Sebastian Sardina talks about his artificial intelligence research at RMIT.

    Transcript

    Dr Sebastian Sardina is a Senior Lecturer/Research Fellow in the Intelligent Agents Group at RMIT University (in the School of Computer Science and IT) in Melbourne, Australia. His research falls in the following sub-areas of Artificial Intelligence: knowledge representation and reasoning, reasoning about action and change, automated planning and intelligent agent systems.

    The video features Dr Sebastian Sardina walking in different areas of the campus, working in a cafe and interacting with students while he narrates over the on screen activity. Upbeat music plays throughout.

    Duration: 46 seconds

    [Opening title]
    Dr Sebastian Sardina
    School of Computer Science and IT
    (Artificial Intelligence)

    I came to RMIT as I wanted to live in Australia and diversify my research in artificial intelligence.

    Besides the quality of life in Melbourne, I stay here for the excellent research environment after enjoying great support during my post-doc position.

    I mostly work on two sub-areas of artificial intelligence.

    I have now been awarded a 3 year research grant for developing techniques to combine lots of small devices into a complex system.

    New techniques in artificial intelligence are making a huge impact within the field of computer science.

    We are now addressing problems that, ten years ago, seemed impossible.

    Being part of this new wave is both inspiring and exciting.

    [Closing credits]
