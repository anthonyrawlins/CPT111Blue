Not a local student?

RMIT considers you a local student if you are:

  • a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • a New Zealand citizen, or
  • a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

You’re viewing program information for international students.

Not an international student?

RMIT considers you an international student if you are:

  • intending to study on a student visa, or
  • not a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • not a New Zealand citizen, or
  • not a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

    Protect industry in a networked world

    Damon Madden completed a Master of Applied Science (Information Security and Assurance) while working full time in the financial sector. He now manages a team of financial crime analysts.

    Job title and organisation: Manager, Crime Detection Analytics
    RMIT program:

    Master of Applied Science (Information Security and Assurance)
    Scholarship or opportunity:
    Research field:
    Country of origin:
    Destination:

    In the information age, criminals will always find a way to circumvent controls and exploit weaknesses in systems, therefore careers in security and financial crime will always be in demand.

    I’ve always had a passion for computing and technology and enjoy problem solving. Security and analytics covers all these bases.

    The program at RMIT combined my interest in mathematics and cryptography with practical topics such as risk management and practical security.

    It was fantastic to have industry professionals guide us through the complexities of penetration testing techniques and the whole experience gave me a greater respect for the white hat hacking community.

    I gained an advanced knowledge of encryption technology along with a better understanding of how these technologies are implemented in real world scenarios.

    Graduates of this program find work in information security, financial crime, security architecture, defence, cryptography and security operations.

    Apply now for midyear: Start your career in Information Security and Assurance.

    Currently, I manage a team of financial crime analysts to create detection scenarios and models across multiple portfolios, with the aim of protecting institutions from financial crime. In addition, I manage the delivery of detection scenarios in line with the institution's anti-money laundering program.

    My studies complemented my role within the bank by giving me practical tools which I have used to further my career.

    It’s competitive to find employment within financial crime, however having completed this degree I now have the necessary qualifications and skills.

    I plan to further develop my security knowledge along with my understanding of the ever-increasing regulatory environment.

    In the information age, criminals will always find a way to circumvent controls and exploit weaknesses in systems, therefore careers in security and financial crime will always be in demand.

    I love what I do because I know I’m doing my bit to protect citizens from crime and criminals alike.

    Vishnupriya Thirumalai studied computer science in India before she decided to pursue further studies in information security at RMIT.

    Job title and organisation:
    RMIT program:

    Master of Applied Science (Information Security and Assurance)
    Scholarship or opportunity:
    Research field:
    Country of origin: India
    Destination:

    I was always attracted to cryptography and the elaborate ways in which secrets were shared.

    I studied a Bachelor of Engineering (Computer Science) in Chennai, India.

    One of the reasons for choosing computer science for my undergraduate degree was to understand how computers shape our lives. I also wanted to contribute to the future of this ever-changing field. When I completed my Engineering degree, I discovered an interest in information security and wanted to pursue further study.

    I was always attracted to cryptography and the elaborate ways in which secrets were shared. I read about cryptography for the first time in a Dan Brown novel, Digital Fortress. It sparked my curiosity and by the time I finished engineering I knew I wanted to study information security.

    While some universities offer intense technical courses, others offer a very small specialisation within a computer science degree. The well-structured program at RMIT includes a good mix of management and technical courses so it was an easy decision for me.

    One of the best things about studying at RMIT is the connections I have made. RMIT’s mentoring program and the industry night in my School have been very useful, with opportunities to interact with industry experts in the field.

    My knowledge about the field has increased tremendously in the past year. I have learnt to manage deadlines well and my analytical know-how has been boosted resulting in improved problem solving skills.

    Postgraduate study is a great way to build on the knowledge acquired during your undergraduate study. It’s certainly the right choice if you want to specialise in a particular field. In my opinion, the best time for postgraduate study is when you have experience and know exactly where you want to be in the industry.

    Information Security roles change often but I believe my degree will help me get to know the industry and face challenging roles. I want to interact with the best information security experts in the field.

    I am motivated, helpful and responsible and I love what I do because it gives me a sense of purpose and it makes me happy.

    With his mind set on a postgraduate degree with a difference, Harshdeep Singh Puri completed a master in Information Security and Assurance.

    Job title and organisation:
    RMIT program:

    Master of Applied Science (Information Security and Assurance)
    Scholarship or opportunity:
    Research field:
    Country of origin: India
    Destination:

    The courses offered within the degree provide the right balance of theory and practice and there is an option for you to specialise in your particular area of interest.

    After completing a bachelor in computer science and engineering I was looking for a specialisation with something new to offer.

    The information security domain is both exciting and challenging, providing a lifelong learning curve. It’s a field that has always fascinated me and now I have the relevant skill set to cater to industry needs.

    RMIT’s Information Security and Assurance master program has a solid reputation. The courses offered within the degree provide the right balance of theory and practice and there is an option for you to specialise in your particular area of interest. In addition, competent and supportive teachers make the job of tackling complex topics a positive experience.

    The program has greatly improved my analytical skills and has helped change my perspective from developer to security analyst. 

    Two of the highlights for me at RMIT were when I received a Student of the Year Award and when I completed a four-month Australian Mathematical Sciences Institute (AMSI) funded internship at PRM Group in Essendon.

    The field of information security is expanding at a rapid pace while providing graduates with plenty of career options, including risk analyst, penetration tester and quality compliance roles.

    The program at RMIT builds a strong foundation for your career in this vast and expanding area. It’s important to have the right mindset in order to tackle what’s ahead in a volatile environment where technologies are constantly changing.

    I’m looking forward to pursuing my career as an operations risk analyst.
