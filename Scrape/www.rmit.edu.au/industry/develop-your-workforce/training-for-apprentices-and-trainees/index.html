Not a local student?

RMIT considers you a local student if you are:

  • a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • a New Zealand citizen, or
  • a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

Not an international student?

RMIT considers you an international student if you are:

  • intending to study on a student visa, or
  • not a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • not a New Zealand citizen, or
  • not a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

Training for apprentices and trainees

Employing an apprentice or trainee is just one way RMIT can develop your workforce.

Why hire apprentices and trainees

You’ll gain the potential to not only improve your bottom line but also spark the exchange of fresh ideas and valuable insights. Australian and Victorian Government incentives are available to eligible employers to reduce training costs. Visit the Australian apprenticeships website for more information.

How to employ apprentices and trainees

There are many ways to find an apprentice including:

  • advertising the position online or in the local paper
  • recruiting someone you know
  • through Group Training Association of Victoria - GTAV
  • contacting Australian Apprenticeship Support Network or Job Services Australia provider.

To sign up your apprentice or trainee, you need to contact an approved Australian Apprenticeship Support Network (AASN), then enrol your apprentice or trainee with a training provider such as RMIT.

Employer responsibilities

As an employer of an apprentice or trainee you need to know your legal rights and obligations.

The RMIT difference

You get more for your business with RMIT. Investing in your apprentice or trainee training can provide your business with real benefits and contribute to your profitability and efficiency.

  • RMIT ensures all training is engaging and challenging for your apprentices and trainees
  • We work with you to ensure a high level of completion rates
  • Apprentices and trainees are trained in line with your business needs
  • You can tour our facilities
  • Access business support for you
  • Mentoring and support for your apprentices and trainees
  • Direct point of contact, to provide you quick and satisfactory customer service
  • Real-time, 24/7 access to the student records of your apprentices and trainees via the VEESS application, which is unique to RMIT.
  • Access to learning, literacy and maths assistance.
  • Personal and career development opportunities, this includes opportunities for involvement in community projects, e.g. working with remote communities.
  • RMIT supports students to enter world skills and other competitions – pathways for career progression training, for you and all employees in your business.

RMIT offers training in the following ways

  • on and off-the-job training and assessment
  • structured classroom delivery
  • a blend of the above
  • block release
  • day release

Cost

The cost of the training is determined upon enrolment. Factors to consider are:

  • if your apprentice or trainee is eligible for a Victorian Government subsidised place and/or if they are eligible for fee concession?
  • is your apprentice or trainee re-commencing an apprenticeship?

You may also be eligible for Australian Government incentives. Discuss this with your Australian Apprenticeship Support Network representative.

If you are interested in sponsoring the fees of your apprentice or trainee, please read and agree to the terms and conditions in the Apprentice/Trainee Sponsorship application form (PDF 126KB).
