Not a local student?

RMIT considers you a local student if you are:

  • a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • a New Zealand citizen, or
  • a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

Not an international student?

RMIT considers you an international student if you are:

  • intending to study on a student visa, or
  • not a citizen or permanent resident of Australia, or
  • not a New Zealand citizen, or
  • not a Temporary Protection visa (TPV) holder.

If you are unsure or hold a different visa type, please contact Info Corner for more information.

On-campus presentations

Make a powerful impression by introducing students to senior executives and alumni who can deliver your message and convey your commitment to recruiting at RMIT.

Come and talk to RMIT students about jobs in your organisation either in person or by video conference. Our on-campus presentations allow you to increase your organisation’s profile and connect directly with our students whilst promoting your development and recruitment programs to potential candidates.

Use your presentation to:

  • showcase your available positions
  • outline your application process
  • share advice and application tips

If you can, bring a recent RMIT graduate who works for your organisation: students are inspired by hearing about our graduates’ successes and experiences.

You are welcome to organise catering - it adds value, particularly at evening sessions.

Our services include:

  • organising a venue on campus in which to hold the presentation
  • promoting the event through CareerHub, targeted emails and social media
  • video conferencing setup
  • catering

Support your presence on campus by lodging your job vacancies on CareerHub.

These sessions can only be attended by RMIT University students. Student ID cards will be scanned upon entry to the venue.

Session dates and times

Sessions are held during teaching periods. We recommend holding your session during the first few weeks of each semester, as students are most active and on campus at that time.

Recommended times:

  • 11.30am–2.30 pm
  • 4.30–6.30 pm
  • 5–8pm (networking events)

How to book

To book an on-campus presentation register now though CareerHub.

Once you have registered or entered your login, select the On-campus presentations booking form link in the left-hand column and complete the form.

Terms and conditions

On-campus presentations are subject to availability and are only for employers currently recruiting.

Bookings need to be made a minimum of seven days before the session date.

We recommended you book up to a month in advance to secure your spot. This free service is subject to availability and RMIT’s discretion.
